All Paws Matter - A dog adoption app

All Paws Matter - An app where you can adopt dogs from voluntary organizations

All Paws Matter began as a research-driven UX project inspired by real-world animal rescue networks operating in regions with limited animal protection. Recognizing that adoption decisions happen in everyday mobile moments, the project evolved into a focused app redesign that simplifies browsing, discovery, and emotional connection between adopters and rescue organizations.

Through user-centered design and refined UI execution, the concept transforms a fragmented Facebook-based process into a clear, compassionate, and accessible mobile experience.

Research Analyze Design

How can I design a software/webpage that can increase the number of adopted dogs, make sure that no dogs get left behind and also keep a close focus on user centered design?

Key Objectives

    Enhance the existing platform (Facebook group) by transitioning to an app solution capable of managing increased traffic and efficiently handling ad postings.

  • Mitigate the impact of Facebook algorithms on ad visibility by implementing a randomized ad refresh feature, ensuring equal exposure for all postings regardless of engagement metrics.
  • Develop a user-friendly interface that provides transparent and up-to-date information on the status of listed dogs, including availability and adoption status, facilitating a seamless user experience.
  • Implement intuitive search and save functionalities within the app, empowering users to easily locate and revisit ads for dogs of interest, thereby enhancing user engagement and satisfaction.

Research Goals

  • Conduct market research to assess demand for a dog adoption service by analyzing current trends and measuring interest within target user groups.
  • Use user research methods such as surveys, interviews, and usability testing to identify needs, pain points, and barriers in the adoption process, and to understand user behavior and preferences.

Secondary Research

Secondary research analyzed dog adoption platforms in Sweden, focusing on usability, information structure, and advertising methods. Key issues included cluttered layouts and poorly organized content, largely due to volunteer-run organizations lacking design resources.

The study also compared animal welfare laws between Sweden and Eastern Europe. Sweden ranks high in protection standards, while countries such as Russia, Belarus, and Ukraine rank significantly lower. This contrast highlighted the scale of the stray dog problem and reinforced the need for improved adoption services in those regions.

Primary Research

''I think I have more faith in a Swedish organization. That the process is more transparent with strict laws. But I haven't really read into it either and would most likely know more the second I start reading into it''

Primary research was conducted through a structured survey to efficiently gather insights from a broad audience within a limited timeframe. Due to project constraints, this method provided the most effective way to collect meaningful data at scale.

The survey was carried out as part of a five-week master’s thesis project and distributed across social media platforms including Facebook and Instagram. It targeted adults over the age of 18 who had considered owning a pet, with the goal of understanding attitudes toward adopting dogs from abroad, identifying key decision factors, and revealing challenges faced by potential adopters.
